Spatial Transcriptomics In Health and Disease: A Stroke Case Study
Spatial transcriptomics maps gene expression within tissues, revealing cellular functions and interactions in their native environment. In this seminar, we'll discuss how we applied this technique alongside single-cell transcriptomics to study molecular responses in mouse brains after ischemic stroke. Our findings show that glial cells—particularly oligodendrocytes—actively participate in glial scar formation and cell communication, challenging their perceived passive role in neuropathologies. Join us to explore how spatial transcriptomics enhances our understanding of complex biological processes and advances stroke research.

(Un)usual characteristics of African mole-rats' oocytes and sex chromosomes through genomics and transcriptomics approaches

Mouse has been used as a classical model to study molecular mechanisms in mammals. However, development of novel low input technologies allowing studying non-model species brought the evidence showing differences compared to mouse. In our laboratory, we study African mole-rats, subterranean rodents with extraordinary characteristics such as long lifespan, very low incidence of cancer and high tolerance or hypoxia. Because of their long female reproductive span, we used them as a model to study long term oocyte and ovarian quality. When we explored characteristics of their oocytes, we unexpectedly revealed that the current model of de novo DNA methylation establishment in mammalian oocytes is restricted to mouse and its close relatives, and a different mechanism takes place in most rodents and other mammals. Moreover, giant mole-rat has unusually large sex chromosomes that evolved through evolutionarily recent translocations from autosomes, allowing us to analyse evolution of sex chromosomes "in real life".

Lenka Gahurová (University of South Bohemia)

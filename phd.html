Doctoral bioinformatics seminar

About

Given the vast diversity within the realm of computational biology, the primary aim of this seminar is to foster interaction among students in the PhD program of Bioinformatics and Computational Biology. The overarching objective is to facilitate a dynamic exchange of knowledge and practices that may be commonplace in one subfield but remain unexplored in another. We strongly believe that by embracing and sharing best practices and experiences across these disciplines, the PhD students can not only excel within their chosen areas of specialization but also transcend the boundaries that separate these fields.
Furthermore, through this collaborative environment, students will gain a unique perspective on the interdisciplinary nature of computational biology. This holistic approach will empower them to not only address the specific challenges within their domain but also find innovative solutions that lie at the intersection of these fields. Ultimately, we envision that this share of ideas and expertise will produce experts capable of pushing the boundaries of knowledge within specific subfields in the landscape of computational biology.
To facilitate the initial exposure to different subfields we have curated a list of recommended resources (see bellow) that encompass the subjects included in our doctoral study program.
The seminar will be held partially together with the bioinformatics seminar for the master students (at least when there are invited lectures.)
